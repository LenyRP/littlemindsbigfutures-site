The Jumpstart Program

What we build in every session

Play-based, structured, and tailored to exactly where your child is right now.

Letter Recognition & Phonics

Learning letter names, letter sounds, and the connection between the two — the foundation everything else in reading is built on.

Listening & Vocabulary

Read-alouds, story retelling, and rich vocabulary exposure — because a child who listens well and knows many words is a child ready to read.

Fine Motor & Pre-Writing

Pencil grip, tracing, cutting, and hand strength activities — the physical foundation that makes writing feel possible instead of frustrating.

Number Operations & Math Foundations

Counting to 20, recognizing numbers, understanding "more" and "less," shapes and patterns — math readiness starts here, not in kindergarten.

Attention & Focus

Building the ability to sit, listen, and engage for progressively longer stretches — one of the most important kindergarten readiness skills, and one of the most overlooked.

Confidence-First Approach

Every session ends with a win — because a 3 or 4-year-old who feels capable is a child who wants to come back. That spark is worth protecting.

Why ages 3–4?

The window you don't want to miss

Between ages 3 and 4, a child's brain is forming neural pathways at a pace it will never again match. This is when curiosity is at its highest, when learning feels like play, and when the patterns established — about reading, about numbers, about what it feels like to try something hard — become the lens a child carries into school.

This isn't about pushing academics onto a toddler. It's about meeting your child in the way they naturally learn — through games, stories, songs, and hands-on exploration — and using that window intentionally. Small investments now return compounding results for years.

"Every child is fearfully and wonderfully made — and a reader in the making. My job at this age is to make sure they know it too."

— Leslie Dykstra, Little Minds, Big Futures
